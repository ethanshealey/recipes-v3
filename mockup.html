Apple Pie

A classic double-crust apple pie with a buttery, flaky pastry and warmly spiced filling. Best served slightly warm.

Prep 30 min
Cook 50 min
Total 1 hr 20 min
Servings
Ingredients
For the crust
2½ cups all-purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 cup unsalted butter, cold & cubed
6 tbsp ice water
For the filling
6 apples, peeled & sliced
¾ cup sugar
2 tbsp cornstarch
1½ tsp cinnamon
¼ tsp nutmeg
Instructions
Make the crust
1.

Whisk flour and salt together in a large bowl. Add cold butter and work it into the flour with your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized pieces remaining.

2.

Drizzle in ice water one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently until the dough just comes together. Divide in half, flatten into discs, wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Make the filling
3.

Toss sliced apples with sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, and nutmeg until evenly coated. Let sit 15 minutes so the apples release their juices.

Assemble & bake
4.

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll one dough disc into a 12-inch circle and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish, letting the excess hang over the edges.

5.

Pour in the filling. Roll the second disc and lay it over the top. Trim the overhang to ½ inch, crimp the edges to seal, and cut several vents in the top crust.

6.

Bake at 425°F for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) and bake another 30–35 minutes until the crust is deep golden and the filling is bubbling.
